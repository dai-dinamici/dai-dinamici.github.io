The **2026 edition** of the online Zoom lecture series [**Computational Methods in Dynamical Systems**](https://sites.google.com/view/dyn-comp-2026/) will take place from **August 31 to September 4, 2026**, as part of the Hokkaido Summer Institute program at Hokkaido University.

The aim of the lecture series is to introduce software libraries and the underlying theory that provide tools for computer-assisted proofs, rigorous computation, and nonrigorous numerical investigation of problems in dynamical systems.

The program will include:

- introductory lectures on dynamical systems and ergodic theory;
- tutorials on the Julia programming language and interval arithmetic libraries;
- methods for computer-assisted proofs; and
- data science methods for the study of dynamical systems.

The lectures will be delivered in English by:

- **Stefano Galatolo**, University of Pisa, Italy *(online)*
- **Isaia Nisoli**, Federal University of Rio de Janeiro, Brazil
- **Warwick Tucker**, Monash University, Australia *(online)*
- **Ippei Obayashi**, Okayama University, Japan
- **Yoshitaka Saiki**, Hitotsubashi University, Japan
- **Yuzuru Sato**, Hokkaido University, Japan

The school is intended for graduate students and researchers from Japan, Australia, European Union, Brazil, and elsewhere.

**Deadline for registration:** Monday, August 24, 2026.
